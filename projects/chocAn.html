About Chocoholic Anonymous

Chocoholic Anonymous refers to a fictitious organization that is dedicated to helping people addicted to Chocolate. This was a Software Engineering course project; the purpose of the project was to iterate through the Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC) and document the phases.

Draw.io was used to draw all the Unified Modelling Language (UML) Diagrams of the Classical Paradigm (e.g. Waterfall SDLC) and the Modern/Object-Oriented Paradigm (e.g. Agile). The following phases of the SDLC were explored:

  • Requirements Phase: This is where the Chocoholic Anonymous concept is explored and refined, and the client’s requirements are drawn out.
  • Analysis (specification) phase: This is where the client’s requirements are analyzed and presented as a specification document that shows what the product is supposed to do.
  • Design Phase: Here, the product as a whole is broken down into components called modules, then each module is designed to show how the product performs its functions.
  • Implementation: Here, black-box testing is used to test the functionalities of Chocoholic Anonymous without needing to know the internal code structure of the product.

Understandably, there was no post-delivery maintenance phase because no changes where expected once the project was submitted.
